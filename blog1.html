Self-Improvement For The Win

Introduction

Do you ever feel like this: “I know what’s wrong with me, and I know what to do about it. Why am I still not able to take action? Wait, what’s actually wrong with me?”. Thus, the thought comes full-circle. As I write this blog, this very thought runs through my mind, and I curse myself for not being able to take meaningful actions towards my goals. But I try to look at the bright side. The very emergence of this thought indicates that I’m somewhat self-aware. If you’re in a similar position, then you have a head start in your self-improvement journey. Let’s understand it better.

The Problem

The two main reasons why we aren’t able take action are:

  • Lack of purpose
  • Lack of discipline / Lack of motivation

Having a “why” is fundamental. If we don’t have a purpose for taking action, we can’t even start. Sure, discipline is key, but that can only come if we have a true sense of purpose. Discipline comes next. For some, it might be interchangeable with motivation, which is the case for me, i.e., I feel discipline creates motivation. But for others, the reverse might be true. Either way, we have to pivot one around the other.

The Solution

Now that we have a clearer idea about the problem, let’s move on to the solution, which is actually simple. Keep in mind that self-improvement is not a goal, it’s a journey, a continuous process. This is where many people falter. We have to learn to enjoy the process more than craving the satisfaction of reaching a goal. This is the only way in which we can ensure that we’re a better version of ourselves today than we were yesterday.
