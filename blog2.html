Emotional Minimalism

Do you know what the chances are of us existing in this world? 1 in 400 trillion, or even less. Yes that’s right! Oh how grateful should we be! Yet, we take most things in our lives for granted. Heck, we take life itself for granted! We allow ourselves to be constantly bombarded by things that are random, and irrelevant to our lives. We then care enough about these things to submit our emotions to them, letting them dictate our mental state.

Understand that there are only two reasons to try to do anything in life: a) because it feels good, or b) because it’s something you think to be good or right. Sometimes these two reasons align. Something feels good AND is that the right thing to try to to and that is just fantastic. But more often, these two things don’t align. Something feels absolutely torturous but is right/good (getting up at 5AM and working out), or something feels amazing but is the bad/wrong thing to do (fill in your own).

Acting based on our feelings is easy. It’s like scratching an itch. There’s a sense of relief that comes along with it. It’s a quick satisfaction. But then that satisfaction is gone just as quickly as it came. Acting based on what’s good/right is difficult. For one, knowing what’s good/right isn’t always straightforward. We probably need to sit down and think hard about it, often having to fight through our lower impulses.

But where do we find the balance? For this we must come back to the simple truth which is that feelings don’t necessarily mean anything. They merely mean whatever you allow them to mean. The point is that you get to decide. And many of us have either forgotten or never realised that fact. And that’s what emotional minimalism is: understanding your emotions and decoupling them from reality.
